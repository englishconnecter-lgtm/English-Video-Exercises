Language focus — advanced structures from the talk

Notice how these C1 patterns are used

Cleft sentence: "It is not nervous energy itself that harms performance — it is how you interpret it."
Fronted adverbial: "On the day of the event, he had help from 41 pacemakers."
Nominalization: "Reframing anxiety as excitement works because both are high-arousal states." (verb → noun phrase as subject)
Concessive clause: "Even the greatest athletes experience negative thoughts — but they have learned to overcome them."

Discussion & extension
